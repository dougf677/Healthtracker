Complete Protein Sources for Muscle Building

Complete proteins provide all nine essential amino acids. For building muscle, it can be useful to include complete or high-quality protein sources regularly across meals.

Animal-based complete proteins

  • Eggs (~3–4 g protein per oz)
  • Greek yogurt (~3 g per oz)
  • Milk (~1 g per oz)
  • Chicken breast (~8–9 g per oz)
  • Turkey (~8 g per oz)
  • Fish (salmon, tuna) (~6–7 g per oz)
  • Lean beef (~7 g per oz)
  • Whey protein (~7–8 g per oz)

Plant options

  • Tofu (~2–3 g per oz)
  • Tempeh (~5 g per oz)
  • Edamame (~3 g per oz)
  • Quinoa (~1–2 g per oz cooked)
  • Chia seeds (~4–5 g per oz)
  • Beans/lentils (~2–3 g per oz cooked)
Simple muscle-building tip: Try to spread protein across the day instead of eating almost all of it in one meal. The calculator’s protein target is a daily estimate; food choices and consistency still matter.

Benefits of Weightlifting and Building Muscle

Strength training, also known as weightlifting, provides a wide range of health benefits beyond just increasing muscle size.

  • Builds muscle strength and lean body mass
  • Helps maintain or increase metabolism, supporting weight management
  • Improves bone density and reduces risk of osteoporosis
  • Enhances balance, coordination, and overall physical function
  • Supports joint health and may reduce risk of injury
  • Can improve mental health, confidence, and overall well-being
  • May help reduce long-term medical expenses by lowering risk of chronic conditions such as heart disease, diabetes, and mobility-related issues

According to the Mayo Clinic, strength training can play an important role in overall health and fitness, helping people stay active and independent as they age.
